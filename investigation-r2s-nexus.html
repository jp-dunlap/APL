Report: The Military-Industrial Complex

The R2S Forward Base: Israel's Arkansas Arsenal

A materialist analysis of the Raytheon-Rafael Strategic Systems (R2S) joint venture in East Camden, Arkansas. The facility is exposed not as a benign economic development project, but as a forward operating base for the Israeli state's military-industrial complex, strategically embedded within the United States to exploit its supply chains, public subsidies, and political cover. This arrangement insulates the Israeli war machine from political pressure while making its armaments eligible for U.S. funding, effectively forcing the American taxpayer to subsidize the occupation of Palestine.

I. Corporate Structure & Genesis

The Raytheon-Rafael Strategic Systems (R2S) joint venture is a masterclass in the architecture of modern imperialist complicity. Its legal and corporate structure is not merely a business arrangement but a political weapon, meticulously engineered to fuse U.S. corporate power with the Israeli state's war-making capacity while providing both with maximum political insulation and profitability.

The Legal Entity: A Shell for Political Insulation

The joint venture is formally constituted as Raytheon RAFAEL Area Protection Systems, LLC. The designation as a Limited Liability Company (LLC) is a deliberate political choice. It creates a legal firewall, establishing a distinct "American" corporate entity that legally separates the U.S. defense behemoth RTX from its partner, Rafael Advanced Defense Systems—an entity wholly owned and controlled by the Israeli state. This obscures the direct fusion of U.S. capital with Israeli state violence, minimizing political risk for RTX.

The Parent Corporations: A Symbiosis of Imperial Power

The R2S venture is a partnership between two pillars of the global military-industrial complex:

  • RTX Corporation: The American partner, the world's largest aerospace and defense contractor and a central node in the U.S. imperialist war machine. The specific business unit driving the venture is its Raytheon division.
  • Rafael Advanced Defense Systems Ltd.: The Israeli partner, a government-owned corporation and a direct instrument of the Israeli state and its Ministry of Defense. Its primary function is to supply advanced weapon systems for the Israeli Occupation Forces (IOF).

This arrangement is not a simple arms sale; it is a strategic fusion. For Rafael, it provides unfettered access to the U.S. supply chain and makes its weapons eligible for U.S. military aid. For RTX, it provides a lucrative product line based on "combat-proven" Israeli technology—a sanitized euphemism for technology tested on Palestinian civilians.

Capitalizing on Conflict

The investment timeline reveals a direct link between conflict and capital. The decision to establish the facility in East Camden was made public in October 2023 with a \$33 million investment. By the groundbreaking in February 2024, the investment had nearly doubled to \$63 million.

This dramatic escalation directly corresponds with the beginning of Israel's intensified genocidal war on Gaza, which saw an unprecedented expenditure of Iron Dome interceptors and a subsequent surge in global demand. The war directly fueled a massive increase in capital investment in Camden. The blood of Palestinians is lubricating the gears of capital accumulation in South Arkansas.

II. The Tamir Missile Supply Chain

The R2S facility is the final node in a logistical network that transforms American components into instruments of Israeli state terror. This reveals the deep, material integration of the American industrial base in the direct arming of the apartheid state.

The Product: A Dual-Use Instrument of Repression

The Camden plant is the first "all-up-round" U.S. production facility for two missile variants based on the same Israeli design:

  • Tamir: The interceptor for Israel's Iron Dome system, used to enforce the siege of Gaza and defend the settler-colonial entity.
  • SkyHunter: The Americanized brand name for the same missile, produced for the U.S. Marine Corps. This dual-production model provides political cover, framing the plant as a critical asset for U.S. national security.
"Made in the USA," Armed by Israel

The Iron Dome is portrayed as a feat of Israeli ingenuity, obscuring its profound dependence on the United States. In reality, the "majority of Tamir missile components are procured through the Raytheon supply chain in the United States." U.S.-made content accounts for roughly 70-75% of the missile, including sophisticated electro-optical sensors, steering fins, and proximity fuzes.

This shatters the propaganda of Israeli self-sufficiency. The Iron Dome is a joint U.S.-Israeli project, dependent on U.S. components and capital—over \$1.6 billion in direct funding from 2011-2021, with another \$1 billion approved in 2022. The Camden plant is where American labor and treasure are converted into the tools of Palestinian oppression.

Logistical Pipeline: From Arkansas to Apartheid

Once assembled, missiles enter a logistical pipeline via U.S. military and commercial transport. Since October 2023, hundreds of U.S. military transport planes have delivered tens of thousands of tons of military equipment to Israel. Commercial carriers like Maersk have been identified transporting military cargo to the Port of Haifa, often originating from U.S. "Strategic Seaports" like JAXPORT, which have become sites of anti-war protest.

III. The Political Architecture

The establishment of the R2S facility was a political project, actively cultivated by a unified bloc of state and federal officials who deliberately chose to tether the Arkansas economy to the Israeli war machine. These political actors do not operate in a vacuum; they are primary targets of and responsive to the state's professional influence industry.

The Champions of Complicity

A cabal of Arkansas politicians provided the political will and public justification for the R2S venture. Key figures include:

  • Governor Sarah Huckabee Sanders (R): The project's chief political sponsor, who personally met with Raytheon executives to "secure this investment" and celebrated the facility for keeping "key allies like Israel safe."
  • Senators John Boozman (R) & Tom Cotton (R): The key federal enablers who provided "unwavering support" and worked to secure federal funding for related military programs.
  • Representatives Bruce Westerman (R) & Steve Womack (R): Provided crucial support in the House, with Womack revealing a statewide network of Iron Dome component manufacturing and receiving \$10,000 from the RTX PAC.

The legislative priorities of these political actors are shaped and reinforced by a powerful lobbying apparatus. For a full analysis of this influence industry, view the report on The Switchboard.

State Subsidies: Public Funds for Private War Profiteering

The R2S project was lured to Arkansas with a generous package of public subsidies administered by the Arkansas Economic Development Commission (AEDC). While the total value is undisclosed, it included infrastructure grants, performance-based incentives (Create Rebate), and sales tax refunds (Tax Back). This system forces Arkansas taxpayers to subsidize a factory co-owned by a foreign government to produce weapons for its military.

Case Study: The State Defends its War Economy

In 2023, just five months before the R2S facility was announced, the Arkansas Department of Finance & Administration (DFA) quietly settled a lawsuit with Raytheon. In the settlement, the DFA agreed to waive over \$3.6 million in penalties and interest that Raytheon owed.

The timing is not coincidental. It is highly probable that the favorable resolution of this tax dispute was a precondition for the R2S deal. The state apparatus acted not as a neutral arbiter but as an active partner, protecting the financial interests of the war industry it so carefully cultivates.

IV. Strategic Analysis & Vulnerabilities

The formidable architecture of complicity surrounding the R2S facility is not without its contradictions. A materialist analysis reveals inherent weaknesses in its structure that present clear and actionable targets for organized resistance.

ANALYSIS: Israel's Forward Operating Base

The R2S facility must be understood as a strategic asset for the Israeli state, established on U.S. soil to achieve three primary objectives:

  • Political Insulation: Wrapping the production of a core Israeli weapon system in the American flag makes it vastly more difficult for activists and principled politicians to oppose, defund, or sanction.
  • Financial Integration: The facility allows the Israeli state to directly tap into U.S. funding streams. The Pentagon's purchase of the American "SkyHunter" variant provides a guaranteed revenue stream that underwrites the entire operation.
  • Logistical Security & Surge Capacity: Establishing a production line within the U.S. guarantees a secure supply chain, immune to blockades. Its function as a surge production site ensures the IOF's ability to sustain high-intensity bombardment.

Identified Vulnerabilities

This interlocking system of power, while formidable, contains inherent weaknesses that can be exploited by organized resistance:

  • Supply Chain Choke Points: The facility is merely an assembly plant, wholly dependent on a deliberately opaque network of U.S. sub-tier suppliers for its core components. These smaller companies are more vulnerable to focused public pressure.
  • Political Patronage: The facility's existence is a direct result of political deals brokered by specific, identifiable politicians. Their political capital is invested in the project's success, making them accountable for its consequences.
  • Labor Contradictions: The defense workforce is subject to corporate exploitation. An active unionization drive by the IAM at a neighboring facility indicates fertile ground for labor organizing and anti-war solidarity.
  • Public Subsidy Racket: The use of public funds to subsidize a private, for-profit venture co-owned by a foreign state is a politically explosive contradiction that can be exposed through FOIA requests and public education campaigns.
