I. The Doctrinal Engine: An Ideology of Colonialism and Annihilation

To understand the political power of Christian Zionism, one must first deconstruct its ideological core. It is not merely a passive theological belief; it is the largest and most powerful pro-Israel movement in the United States, and it functions as an essential ideological pillar of the broader U.S. Christian Right. Its political force is derived from a specific and highly influential End Times theology with deep roots in 16th-century European Protestantism, a framework that reflects the colonial and imperial context in which it was formed. This doctrine, known as Dispensational Premillennialism, posits that human history is divided into distinct eras or "dispensations," culminating in the Second Coming of Christ.

Within this framework, the modern state of Israel plays a central and non-negotiable role. Christian Zionists believe that the gathering of Jewish people in historic Palestine and their establishment of a sovereign state are the direct fulfillment of biblical prophecy—a necessary precondition for the final sequence of apocalyptic events. This sequence includes the Rapture, the Tribulation, the Battle of Armageddon, and the ultimate return of Christ to establish a thousand-year kingdom from Jerusalem. This theological framework is inherently anti-Palestinian, as it views any challenge to absolute Jewish control over historic Palestine as a satanic obstruction to the divine prophetic timeline. The existence, rights, and history of the Palestinian people—including Palestinian Christians—are rendered theologically irrelevant, an obstacle to be overcome in the pursuit of a divine mandate.

Furthermore, this ideology is fundamentally and profoundly antisemitic. It objectifies Jewish people, reducing them to mere instruments in a Christian eschatological drama. In this worldview, the state of Israel and the Jewish people are not valued for their own sake, but are instead cast as pawns whose prophesied role is to facilitate a Christian apocalypse. The ultimate fate of Jewish people in this theology is either conversion to Christianity or eternal damnation in a lake of fire alongside all other non-believers. This "philosemitism"—a fetishized and conditional love for a "biblical Israel"—provides a powerful moral cover for a deeply antisemitic and colonialist political project. In Arkansas, this doctrine is the engine that drives a sophisticated political machine, providing a divine mandate for policies that support the Israeli state, regardless of their devastating human cost.

II. The Arkansas Command & Control Apparatus: An Integrated System of Influence

The power of the Christian Zionist movement in Arkansas is not diffuse or accidental; it is highly concentrated and strategically deployed through a sophisticated and vertically integrated institutional triad. This network was architected by its central charismatic figure, Pastor Howard "Happy" Caldwell, and functions as a cohesive, self-reinforcing system for recruitment, indoctrination, and the projection of political influence from the local to the national level.

The Charismatic Architect: Pastor Howard "Happy" Caldwell

The network's foundational narrative and charismatic authority emanate from its founder. The official history, consistently repeated across all organizational platforms, is that in 1979, "God spoke to him to build a spiritual production center" in Little Rock. This claim of direct divine commission is central to his authority, positioning him not merely as an organizational leader but as a prophetic figure whose vision is divinely ordained and thus non-negotiable for his followers. His influence is further bolstered by a carefully cultivated public profile, including an honorary Doctor of Divinity from Oral Roberts University and a 2005 invitation to the US Army War College Strategic Leader Staff Ride at Gettysburg, which signifies a cultivated relationship with secular and military leadership circles, lending his ministry a veneer of mainstream legitimacy.

The Institutional Triad: A Vertically Integrated Machine

The power of the Caldwell network derives from a highly integrated institutional structure that controls every stage of the influence pipeline, from initial indoctrination to national-level political action:

  • Agape Church: The Organizing and Recruitment Base. Located in Little Rock, Agape Church serves as the physical and spiritual center of gravity for the entire network. It provides the manpower, community, and financial base for the organization's activities and functions as a platform for political cultivation. Its influence is institutionalized through a cradle-to-adulthood educational pipeline, comprising Agape Academy (K-12) and Agape College. This pipeline ensures ideological reproduction, training the next generation of leaders and activists within a closed, self-referential system.
  • Victory Television Network (VTN): The Propaganda Arm. Explicitly defined as the "broadcasting arm of the Little Rock–based Agape Church," VTN operates a network of three full-power television stations reaching a potential 1.2 million households across Arkansas. Its programming schedule is a curated ideological echo chamber, featuring Caldwell's own programs alongside national Christian Zionist figures like CUFI founder John Hagee and programs like Jerusalem Dateline, which offers a "biblical and prophetic perspective" on Middle East news. As a non-profit, VTN's 2023 IRS filings show revenues of \$2.14 million and total assets of \$9.87 million, demonstrating a significant operational capacity to disseminate its message to a mass audience.
  • National Integration via CUFI: The Force Multiplier. The Arkansas-based apparatus is fully integrated with the national Christian Zionist movement through Christians United for Israel (CUFI), the largest pro-Israel organization in the United States with a claimed 10 million members. Happy Caldwell is not a passive member but a key leader within CUFI's national command structure, holding the dual positions of Regional Director and a member of its Executive Board. This provides a direct, two-way channel of influence. National priorities and legislative templates from CUFI are transmitted down to Caldwell for effective implementation in Arkansas, while the Arkansas network's success provides Caldwell with a seat at the table in national decision-making.

Case Study: The Lawfare of Senator Bart Hester & The Passage of Act 710

The most significant and consequential victory for the Christian Zionist apparatus in Arkansas is the passage of Act 710 of 2017, the state's draconian anti-BDS law. The law's primary sponsor, Senator Bart Hester, provided a direct and unambiguous confession of its theological intent during the filming of the 2021 documentary "Boycott," proving that the legislation was an act of religious doctrine, not a piece of economic policy.

"I believe in the Bible, so I absolutely believe the Jewish people are God's chosen people and therefore I have a responsibility to benefit them where possible... There is going to be certain things that happen in Israel before Christ returns. There will be famines and disease and war. And the Jewish people are going to go back to their homeland. At that point Jesus Christ will come back to the earth. Anybody, Jewish or not Jewish, that doesn't accept Christ, in my opinion, will end up going to hell."

- Senator Bart Hester, Primary Sponsor of Act 710

Hester's statement is a smoking gun. It is a direct admission that he used his public office as a state legislator to codify his specific, apocalyptic End Times theology into the laws of Arkansas, forcing all state contractors—regardless of their own beliefs—to comply with his religious mandate. This represents a profound capture of the legislative process for ideological ends, transforming a theological belief into a coercive instrument of state power. View the full report on Act 710.

Case Study: The State-Sanctioned Propaganda of Rep. Mindy McAlindon

In April 2023, the Arkansas House of Representatives passed a non-binding resolution, sponsored by Rep. Mindy McAlindon, that provides another clear example of the legislature being used as a platform for Christian Zionist propaganda. The resolution formally declares that "the State of Arkansas, which lies in America’s heartland, has a special kinship with Judea and Samaria, Israel’s biblical heartland."

The use of the term "Judea and Samaria"—the biblical name for the occupied West Bank favored by the illegal settler movement—is a deliberate political act of erasure. It is an attempt to supplant the internationally recognized name of Palestine with a biblical one, thereby denying Palestinian history, identity, and claim to the land. The resolution was drafted in direct consultation with Yossi Dagan, the head of the Samaria Regional Council and a key figure in the illegal settlement enterprise. This action uses the official imprimatur of the Arkansas state legislature to legitimize illegal settlements and erase the Palestinian presence from the land, directly translating a biblical narrative into an official state proclamation at the behest of the settler movement itself.

Case Study: The Agape Pipeline and the Capture of the State Treasury

The network's strategy extends beyond influencing legislation to the direct cultivation and installation of its members into key positions of state power. Arkansas State Treasurer John Thurston is a direct product of this system. He is a graduate of Agape College and, according to his official biography, worked for 13 years in "ministry operations at Agape Church" before launching his political career. A 2014 campaign finance report shows a direct financial contribution from "Agape Church" to Thurston's campaign.

This is not a mere personal connection; it is a model of elite capture. The Agape network successfully created a political pipeline, moving one of its own long-term operatives from its internal ministry directly into a constitutional office overseeing the state's finances. This provides the network with a powerful and sympathetic ally inside the executive branch, ensuring that the state's financial policies are aligned with its ideological agenda.
