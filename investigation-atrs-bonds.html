Definitive Report: Financial Complicity & Political Capture

The Pension Pipeline: A Trust Betrayed

A definitive investigation into the Arkansas Teacher Retirement System (ATRS) that exposes how the $22 billion public pension fund was captured by a right-wing political influence campaign. This report deconstructs, with documentary evidence, how State Auditor Dennis Milligan, operating as a key agent of a national network, orchestrated the diversion of public funds into high-risk Israeli bonds, flagrantly violating state law and breaching the sacred fiduciary duty owed to every Arkansas educator, past and present.

I. The Anatomy of Political Capture

The ATRS Board's decision on June 2, 2025, to invest $50 million in Israeli sovereign bonds was not a prudent financial decision born of independent analysis; it was the calculated result of a deliberate political operation. This campaign, architected and driven by State Auditor Dennis Milligan, represents a flagrant violation of his fiduciary duty—a duty strictly limited by Arkansas law to "pecuniary factors" only. Intelligence confirms Milligan, acting as a key operative for the State Financial Officers Foundation (SFOF), a national right-wing network, weaponized his public office to divert pension funds to a foreign state for purely ideological reasons. This operation willfully ignored concurrent credit downgrades and explicit warnings of high financial risk from S&P Global Ratings, demonstrating a reckless disregard for the financial security of Arkansas teachers.

The Influence Campaign: Actors and Strategy

The capture of the ATRS was not an isolated event but a coordinated effort involving multiple actors executing a clear strategy. Understanding these components is key to understanding the breach of trust.

The Architect: Auditor Dennis Milligan

As an ex-officio member of the ATRS Board, Milligan was uniquely positioned to act as the conduit for an external political agenda. Email evidence proves his office, led by Chief Deputy Auditor Jason Brady, personally initiated, facilitated, and drove the campaign to divert ATRS funds. This was not the action of a neutral trustee but of a political operative with a long-standing ideological commitment to this specific cause.

The Network: The State Financial Officers Foundation (SFOF)

The SFOF functions as a national coordination hub for right-wing state financial officers. Deeply intertwined with ALEC and the Heritage Foundation, it provides the strategy, talking points, and political cover for officials like Milligan to combat "woke capitalism." The ATRS investment is a textbook execution of the SFOF script, which explicitly links anti-ESG campaigns with pro-Israel advocacy.

The Strategy: A "Pincer Movement"

The campaign's brilliance lies in its two-pronged attack, executed in the same ATRS board meeting to achieve total ideological alignment:

  • First Pincer (Political Capital Transfer): Force the ATRS to purchase $50 million in high-risk, unrated Israel Bonds. This funnels public funds to a foreign state for ideological reasons while ignoring clear, negative credit warnings from S&P and Moody's.
  • Second Pincer (Systemic Deregulation): Simultaneously approve the hiring of Egan-Jones Proxy Services. The firm's "Wealth-Focused Policy" explicitly votes *against* shareholder proposals seeking corporate accountability on human rights, environmental damage, and political lobbying.

This reveals the core hypocrisy of the operation: using public funds to support a political cause while simultaneously using the immense power of those same funds to dismantle corporate accountability and silence shareholder voices on matters of justice.

II. The Breach of Fiduciary Duty: A Definitive Legal Analysis

The actions of the ATRS Board and its political influencers do not exist in an ethical gray area. They represent a clear, demonstrable, and multi-faceted breach of the legal duties imposed upon them by Arkansas state law. This analysis deconstructs each specific violation.

LEGAL ANALYSIS: VIOLATION OF ARKANSAS CODE § 24-2-804(a)

The law is unambiguous. It requires that public pension investment decisions be based *solely* on "pecuniary factors"—that is, factors having a material effect on financial risk or return. The law explicitly forbids fiduciaries from furthering "social, political, or ideological goals".

Breach 1: Violation of the Duty of Loyalty

The duty of loyalty requires trustees to act with undivided fidelity to the beneficiaries. The evidence proves the Board acted in the interest of an external political agenda. The entire operation, from Milligan's politically-motivated initiation to the victory lap email sent to the SFOF, proves the decision was driven by non-pecuniary political factors, violating the duty to act solely in the interest of the beneficiaries.

Breach 2: Violation of the Duty of Prudence

The duty of prudence requires "reasonable care, skill, and caution." The Board failed this standard by willfully ignoring clear pecuniary risk factors—multiple credit downgrades from S&P Global. Furthermore, they circumvented their own governing policy (Board Policy 4) which requires a written recommendation from an investment consultant *before* an investment is made. This inversion of process is the hallmark of imprudent decision-making.

Breach 3: Improper Delegation and Abdication

By hiring Egan-Jones, the Board improperly delegated its duty of analysis. Instead of performing the case-by-case analysis required by law to determine if an ESG issue is a "pecuniary factor," they outsourced their voting to a firm whose entire business model is to *not* perform that analysis and to vote against such proposals on ideological grounds. This is an abdication of their core fiduciary responsibility.

The political capture of teachers' retirement funds is not just unethical; it is a direct violation of the legal standards enacted to protect them. While legal challenges by members may be complex, the basis for an investigation by the State Attorney General is clear, compelling, and documented.

V. Recommendations for Action

The evidence leads to an unequivocal conclusion: the Arkansas Teacher Retirement System has been subjected to a successful political capture campaign. This represents a profound betrayal of public trust and jeopardizes the integrity of a system upon which tens of thousands of public servants depend. To address these serious breaches, the following actions are recommended.

1. Investigation by the Arkansas Attorney General

The evidence of a coordinated political campaign, the apparent violation of state fiduciary laws (specifically Act 498), and the circumvention of governance policies warrant a formal investigation by the Arkansas Attorney General to determine if state ethics or fiduciary laws were broken. This is the most direct path to official accountability.

2. Public Awareness & Member Mobilization

ATRS members must be made aware of this breach of trust. A public education campaign targeting educators, the Arkansas Education Association (AEA), and other public sector unions should be launched to expose how their retirement funds were used for political ends, demanding accountability from the ATRS Board and the political actors involved.

3. Policy Reform to Prevent Recurrence

The ATRS Board must amend its policies to require that any investment proposed by a Board member, particularly an ex-officio political appointee, be subject to a mandatory, independent fiduciary analysis *before* it can be placed on an agenda. The Board must also reverse its decision to hire Egan-Jones and develop a proxy voting policy that is truly compliant with state law, requiring case-by-case analysis of material risks.
