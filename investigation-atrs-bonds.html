Report: Financial Complicity

The Pension Fund Pipeline

The Arkansas Teacher Retirement System (ATRS), entrusted with the retirement security of public educators, channels significant capital into a global financial system that sustains and profits from the Israeli apartheid regime. This dossier maps the pipeline through which teachers' deferred wages are invested in corporations that arm, supply, and support the ongoing occupation of Palestine.

I. Executive Summary & Strategic Overview

On June 2, 2025, the Arkansas Teacher Retirement System (ATRS) Board of Trustees passed Resolution 2025-22, authorizing an investment of up to $50 million in sovereign bonds issued by the State of Israel. Documents obtained via the Freedom of Information Act reveal this decision was not a routine, apolitical financial determination based solely on "pecuniary factors" as required by state law. Instead, it was the culmination of a coordinated, multi-pronged political influence campaign orchestrated by the office of Arkansas State Auditor Dennis Milligan. Milligan, a key figure in the State Financial Officers Foundation (SFOF)—a national network of right-wing financial officials dedicated to combating "woke capitalism"—leveraged his political office to advance a specific geopolitical agenda.

This meticulously documented campaign pressured the ATRS leadership, secured a third-party manager (Scout Investments/Reams Asset Management) at a remarkably low fee of 3 basis points (0.03%) to neutralize financial objections, and deployed hasbara (propaganda) from the "Israel Bonds" organization that directly contradicts independent, contemporaneous credit risk assessments from S&P Global.

Simultaneously, and in a move of profound strategic importance, the ATRS Board also voted to consolidate its proxy voting power under a new firm, Egan-Jones. The "Wealth-Focused Policy" of Egan-Jones explicitly opposes shareholder proposals related to environmental, social, and human rights issues. This parallel maneuver creates a powerful synergy of reaction: while ATRS capital is used to directly fund an apartheid state actively engaged in genocide, its immense power as a shareholder will be systematically used to shield corporations from accountability on the very issues of justice, labor, and human rights that the state of Israel violates with impunity. This analysis deconstructs the timeline, identifies the network of actors, exposes the deceptive financial arguments, and situates this decision within the broader political struggle against imperialism and settler-colonialism.

II. The Milligan Doctrine: Anatomy of a Political Operation

The $50 million investment in Israeli sovereign bonds was not a case of passive complicity or poor judgment. It was the result of a deliberate, coordinated political influence campaign, architected by State Auditor Dennis Milligan. This operation represents a flagrant violation of his fiduciary duty to the pensioners of Arkansas—a duty strictly limited by state law to "pecuniary factors" only. Intelligence confirms Milligan, acting as a key operative of a national right-wing network, weaponized his public office to divert pension funds to a foreign state for purely ideological reasons, willfully ignoring concurrent credit downgrades and explicit warnings of high financial risk.

Jason Brady, on behalf of State Auditor Dennis Milligan, emails the executive directors of ATRS (Mark White) and APERS (Amy Fecher). The subject: "Israel bonds." The email states, "The Auditor has been contacted by a representative of Israel Bonds and asked if he would arrange a meeting." This frames the interaction as a political directive, not a standard financial pitch.

November 2024 - April 2025: The Political "Playbook"

Auditor Milligan's office acts as a concierge for "Israel Bonds" agents Larry Berman and Brad Young, scheduling a series of meetings with influential Arkansas figures, including legislative leadership (Hester, Evans), financial officers (Thurston, Hudson), and the primary pension fund targets (White, Fecher).

S&P Global lowers Israel's long-term sovereign credit rating to 'A+' and assigns a 'Negative Outlook,' citing "heightened geopolitical risk." This material event directly contradicts the "safe investment" narrative being pushed by the Bonds organization.

May 8, 2025: Directive Issued

Less than a month after meeting with the Israeli agents, Mark White internally directs his deputy to "move forward with a proposal to the Board in June," signaling the decision is a foregone conclusion.

May 28, 2025: The Financial Enabler is Secured

Aon consultant PJ Kelly forwards an email from Reams Asset Management offering to manage the mandate for a fee of just 3 basis points (0.03%). Kelly notes Reams is "light on experience with Israel bonds," revealing expertise was secondary to securing a compliant manager at a nominal cost.

June 2, 2025: The Pincer Movement Succeeds

The ATRS Board approves Resolution 2025-22, authorizing the $50 million investment. In the same meeting, they approve Resolution 2025-21 to hire Egan-Jones Proxy Services, whose official policy opposes shareholder proposals on human rights and environmental issues, thus consolidating financial and proxy power in alignment with a right-wing agenda.

The Legal Vulnerability: Breach of Fiduciary Duty

ANALYSIS: VIOLATION OF STATE LAW

Arkansas Code Ann. § 24-2-802 is unambiguous. It requires that public pension investment decisions be based *solely* on "pecuniary factors"—that is, factors having a material effect on financial risk or return. The law explicitly forbids fiduciaries from considering or acting to further "social, political, or ideological goals."

The entire operation, from Milligan's politically-motivated initiation to the willful disregard of negative credit ratings from Moody's and S&P Global, proves the decision was driven by non-pecuniary factors. This represents a textbook breach of fiduciary duty under Arkansas law, creating a clear basis for legal action to hold the trustees accountable and protect pensioners' capital from politically motivated speculation.

III. The Corporate Complicity Pipeline

The Mechanisms of Complicity

ATRS's complicity is structured primarily through its holdings in broad market index funds and other pooled investment vehicles managed by global asset management firms like BlackRock and Loomis Sayles. This passive investment strategy leads to a channeling of capital into companies involved in human rights abuses, often without active ethical decision-making. This "complicity by default" is a structural feature of modern finance that does not absolve the investor of responsibility.

While Arkansas Act 411 mandates that ATRS investment decisions be based on "pecuniary factors" only, this creates a potential "accountability gap" where the fund remains financially linked to companies involved in severe human rights controversies, as these violations are not yet universally priced in as material financial risks.
