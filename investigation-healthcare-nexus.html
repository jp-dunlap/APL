Report: Healthcare & Financial Complicity

The Healthcare-Apartheid Nexus

A systemic entanglement of Arkansas's healthcare and pension systems with the economy of the Israeli apartheid state and its supporting military-industrial complex. This investigation reveals a deeply embedded complicity that extends from pharmaceutical procurement and financial investments to academic partnerships, exposing the mechanisms through which Arkansas institutions contribute to and profit from oppression.

I. The Pharmaceutical Pipeline: Subsidizing Corporate Power

The flow of capital from Arkansas's healthcare sector to Israeli corporate interests, particularly Teva Pharmaceuticals, represents a significant form of complicity. Seemingly routine mechanisms of pharmaceutical procurement—state formularies, hospital purchasing, and major insurance payers—create a systemic pipeline that channels Arkansas healthcare dollars towards an Israeli multinational implicated in the economic structures of occupation.

The State of Arkansas Formulary guides drug selection and reimbursement, creating a direct channel for public and private healthcare funds to benefit Teva Pharmaceuticals, an Israeli multinational. The inclusion of these drugs ensures a steady revenue stream from Arkansas to Teva.

Teva's Operational Complicity in Occupied Palestine

Teva systematically benefits from the Israeli occupation by exploiting a "captive Palestinian market." This is facilitated by the Paris Protocol, which subordinates the Palestinian economy to Israeli control, granting Teva unimpeded market access while severely restricting Palestinian pharmaceutical companies. This controlled environment shields Teva from competition and allows it to profit directly from the structures of apartheid, making any revenue from Arkansas a contribution to a corporation embedded in occupation.

II. The Portfolio of Complicity: Profiting From War

The financial arteries of Arkansas's most prominent public and private institutions are demonstrably intertwined with corporations that fuel and profit from the Israeli military apparatus. This section uncovers the investments held by institutions dedicated to healing, education, and public service, exposing a profound ethical dissonance.

The Mechanism: Complicity by Default

Institutions like the UAMS Foundation, Baptist Health Foundation, and the Arkansas Teacher Retirement System (ATRS) manage billions of dollars in endowments and pension funds. To achieve diversification and market returns, they invest heavily in broad-market index funds and mutual funds managed by global asset firms like BlackRock and State Street.

This standard investment practice creates a "complicity by default." Even without a direct decision to invest in a specific defense contractor, their capital is funneled into the very corporations that arm, supply, and technologically enable the occupation of Palestine. The largest and most popular index funds (e.g., those tracking the S&P 500) are saturated with these complicit firms.

The Evidence Locker: Documented Complicit Holdings

The following are key corporate holdings commonly found in the large-cap index funds used by institutional investors. These firms are directly complicit in violations of international law and Palestinian human rights.

The Boeing Company

Nature of Complicity: A principal supplier of combat aircraft, bombs, and missiles to the Israeli military.

Human Rights Watch and Amnesty International have documented the use of Boeing-manufactured GBU-39 Small Diameter Bombs and JDAM guidance kits in unlawful Israeli airstrikes on Gaza that have killed civilians and destroyed residential buildings.

Alphabet Inc. (Google)

Nature of Complicity: Provider of AI and cloud computing services to the Israeli government and military through the controversial "Project Nimbus" contract.

The Intercept has reported that Project Nimbus provides the Israeli military with advanced capabilities like facial detection, automated image categorization, and object tracking, which can be used for surveillance and targeting of Palestinians.

Elbit Systems Ltd.

Nature of Complicity: Israel's largest private arms manufacturer and a key supplier of drones, surveillance technology, and artillery systems to the IDF.

Elbit's Hermes drones are extensively used for surveillance and targeted assassinations in Gaza. The company also provides surveillance equipment for Israel's apartheid wall and technology for its armored vehicles. Multiple international pension funds have divested from Elbit due to its role in human rights violations.

III. The Normalization Apparatus: Masking Politics with Science

Beyond direct financial flows, a more insidious form of complicity operates within Arkansas's academic and research spheres. Partnerships between Arkansas institutions and Israeli entities, presented under the neutral banner of "scientific progress," serve to normalize and legitimize the Israeli state, whitewashing its image while ignoring the systemic oppression of Palestinians.

Case Study: UAMS and the Israel Cancer Research Fund (ICRF)

The public association between UAMS and the ICRF, which exclusively funds cancer research within Israel, lends UAMS's institutional prestige to an Israeli national fund. This engagement occurs in a political vacuum, ignoring the severe restrictions faced by Palestinian academics and scientists. The shared commitment to fighting cancer is used to overshadow the realities of apartheid, thereby normalizing the Israeli state as a legitimate international partner.

Case Study: The BIRD Foundation Subsidy Pipeline

A \$2.2 million grant from the U.S.-Israeli BIRD Foundation directly connects the University of Arkansas system to the Israeli security state. The grant partners Bastazo, a UofA startup, with Salvador Technologies, an Israeli cybersecurity firm whose founders are veterans of the IDF's elite intelligence and cyber warfare units (including Unit 8200).

The BIRD Foundation, co-managed by the U.S. Department of Homeland Security and the Israel National Cyber Directorate, uses U.S. taxpayer money to provide risk-free grants to these partnerships. This mechanism functions as a state-corporate welfare pipeline, using the University of Arkansas as a legitimizing vehicle to channel public funds to a private extension of a foreign military-intelligence apparatus.
