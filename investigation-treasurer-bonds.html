Report: Financial Complicity

The Treasurer's War Chest: An Analysis of Arkansas State Funds & The Israel Bond Portfolio

This report provides a forensic analysis of the Arkansas State Treasurer's Office as a primary actor in the state's financial complicity with the Israeli apartheid regime, focusing on the direct management of general state funds.

I. Introduction: Beyond the Pension Fund

While the political capture of the Arkansas Teacher Retirement System (ATRS) is a critical battlefield, it is not the only front in the war against financial complicity. The Arkansas State Treasurer's Office itself operates as a direct and willing conduit for funneling public money into the Israeli war machine. This analysis, based on documents obtained via the Freedom of Information Act, reveals that the state's $52 million investment in Israel Bonds is not a passive or accidental holding, but a deliberate policy choice made by the state's chief financial officers in defiance of their fiduciary duties.

II. Forensic Financial Analysis

A detailed review of the state's financial records exposes a portfolio that is both politically motivated and financially unsound. The Treasurer's office has directed millions of dollars of public money into these high-risk assets, even as their value has declined.

Documented Financial Detriment

The state's own records demonstrate the poor performance of these assets. As of October 7, 2024, the Israel Bond portfolio showed a total unrealized loss of $(191,732.00). This financial detriment directly contradicts any argument that these bonds are a uniquely safe or profitable investment for the state.

The Treasurer's office has actively increased this exposure. A wire transfer confirmation from November 2023 shows a $5 million purchase, and an order form from May 5, 2025, documents another $10 million investment, with the "Arkansas State Treasurer" listed as the purchaser. These transactions confirm the Treasurer's office as the direct operational agent of the state's complicity.

III. The Fiduciary Contradiction

The state's decision to not only hold but actively increase its investment in Israel Bonds represents a gross dereliction of fiduciary duty. This duty legally and ethically obligates public officials to act with undivided loyalty and prudence, solely in the financial interest of the state's taxpayers. The evidence shows they have failed this standard.

ANALYSIS: Willful Negligence of Material Risk

The world's leading credit rating agencies issued clear and unambiguous warnings about the financial risks associated with Israel's economy, directly tied to its escalating military actions.

  • S&P Global Ratings (October 1, 2024): Downgraded Israel's credit rating to 'A' with a "negative outlook," citing "Increased security risks due to the escalation of conflict with Hezbollah and recent military activities in Gaza."
  • Moody's Ratings (September 27, 2024): Downgraded Israel's credit rating to 'Baa1' and maintained a "negative outlook," citing the same geopolitical risks.

These are material risk factors that any responsible fiduciary would be obligated to consider. Yet, the Treasurer's office proceeded with a $10 million bond purchase in May 2025, months *after* these downgrades were made public. The provided FOIA documents contain **no evidence** of any formal risk assessment or discussion by the Treasurer's office or the State Board of Finance regarding these explicit warnings. This is not just poor judgment; it is willful negligence.

IV. Conclusion: Politics as Fiscal Policy

The investment strategy of the Arkansas State Treasurer's Office is not being driven by sound financial management. It is being driven by politics. The decision to purchase and hold $52 million in high-risk, underperforming Israel Bonds, in direct contradiction of expert financial warnings, can only be understood as an act of political solidarity disguised as fiscal policy.

This strategy appears to be a direct implementation of the political mandate established by **Act 710 of 2017**, the state's anti-BDS law. This law created a political imperative to support Israel, and the Treasurer's office is using public funds to fulfill that imperative, regardless of the financial cost to the taxpayers of Arkansas. The gross mismanagement of public funds in service of a foreign state's political agenda represents a profound betrayal of the public trust.
