Report: The Military-Industrial Complex

The R2S Kill Chain: A Supply Web Analysis

A deep-dive forensic analysis identifying the top U.S.-based sub-tier suppliers for Israel's Tamir missile. This report maps the specific corporate nodes, high-technology components, and logistical chokepoints that form the material foundation of the Raytheon-Rafael Strategic Systems (R2S) facility in Camden, Arkansas, transforming an abstract understanding of the military supply chain into an actionable map of its decentralized, material components.

I. Executive Summary: The Anatomy of the Kill Chain

This investigation confirms that the R2S facility in Camden is the terminal point of a vast, interdependent, and geographically dispersed sub-tier supply web. The production of Tamir and SkyHunter missiles is wholly dependent on a network of specialized U.S. corporations that provide the most critical, high-technology components. This report identifies the five most indispensable suppliers whose products constitute the core of the missile's functionality: the propulsion, guidance, and ordnance systems. Exposing this network decentralizes the struggle against the Camden facility, creating new fronts for political action, public pressure, and labor organizing at more vulnerable corporate nodes across the United States.

II. Top 5 Critical Sub-Tier Supplier Dossiers

The following dossiers provide a detailed analysis of the corporate entities that form the indispensable foundation of the R2S-Camden kill chain. These companies are not peripheral actors; they are the manufacturers of the core technologies that enable the Tamir and SkyHunter missile systems to function.

1. L3Harris Technologies: The Propulsion & Fuzing Nexus

Role in Kill Chain: Primary supplier of Solid Rocket Motors (SRMs) and Proximity Fuzing Systems.

L3Harris is arguably the most critical single node in the supply chain. Its Aerojet Rocketdyne division, with its "Center of Excellence" located directly within the Camden defense hub, is a dominant force in missile propulsion. Furthermore, its facility in Cincinnati, OH, is a leading producer of proximity fuzes, the critical technology that allows an interceptor to detonate near its target without direct impact.

  • Key Facilities: Camden, AR (SRMs); Cincinnati, OH (Fuzing Systems).
  • Identified Vulnerability: The Camden SRM facility is the target of an active unionization drive by the International Association of Machinists and Aerospace Workers (IAMAW), representing a significant labor-based chokepoint.

2. Northrop Grumman: The Integrated Systems Provider

Role in Kill Chain: Supplier of SRMs, EO/IR Sensors, Fuzing Systems, and Composite Missile Airframes.

Northrop Grumman possesses the industrial capacity to supply nearly every critical subsystem of the missile. Its deep integration across the defense sector makes it a foundational pillar of the entire U.S. missile production base. The company's advanced composite manufacturing capabilities, likely centered at its Iuka, MS, facility, are essential for producing the lightweight, durable airframes required for high-performance interceptors.

  • Key Facilities: Rocket Center, WV (SRMs); Rolling Meadows, IL (Sensors); Iuka, MS (Composites).

3. Moog Inc.: The Guidance & Control Specialist

Role in Kill Chain: Near-sole-source provider of Fin Control Actuation Systems (FCAS).

Moog is a highly specialized and indispensable supplier. The FCAS is the electromechanical system that translates the missile's digital guidance commands into the precise physical movements of its steering fins. Without this technology, the missile cannot be guided. Moog's market dominance in this niche, high-precision field makes it a critical and potentially irreplaceable chokepoint in the supply chain.

  • Key Facilities: Blacksburg, VA; Murphy, NC.

4. Leonardo DRS: The Eyes of the Missile

Role in Kill Chain: Key provider of advanced Electro-Optical/Infrared (EO/IR) Sensors and Targeting Systems.

Leonardo DRS specializes in the sophisticated sensors that function as the missile's "eyes," allowing it to detect and track targets. The Tamir missile relies on an EO/IR seeker to lock onto its target in the terminal phase of an intercept. The advanced sensors produced by firms like Leonardo DRS are a core enabling technology for the entire Iron Dome system.

  • Key Facility: Melbourne, FL.

5. BAE Systems: The Energetics Foundation

Role in Kill Chain: Foundational supplier of Propellants and Explosives.

BAE Systems, through its role as the operating contractor for the U.S. Army's Holston (Tennessee) and Radford (Virginia) ammunition plants, is the foundational supplier of the energetic materials for the entire kill chain. These Government-Owned, Contractor-Operated (GOCO) facilities produce the essential propellants required by SRM manufacturers like L3Harris and Northrop Grumman. Without BAE's production, there is no propulsion.

  • Key Facilities: Holston Army Ammunition Plant, TN; Radford Army Ammunition Plant, VA.

III. Strategic Analysis: A Decentralized Network of Vulnerability

The mapping of the R2S supply web reveals a profound strategic vulnerability. The production of Israel's premier missile defense system is not a self-contained operation within a hardened Arkansas facility; it is a decentralized process critically dependent on a handful of specialized corporate actors located across the politically contested terrain of the United States.

ANALYSIS: The Chokepoints of Production

This intelligence fundamentally alters the strategic calculus for anti-war and Palestine solidarity movements. It provides a concrete, evidence-based list of new corporate targets that are more accessible and potentially more susceptible to pressure than the prime contractor, RTX. The key vulnerabilities are:

  • Geographic Decentralization: The struggle against the Camden facility can now be waged in Florida, Virginia, North Carolina, Ohio, and Mississippi. This allows for the mobilization of local and regional allies, transforming a single-point campaign into a distributed, nationwide effort.
  • Labor as a Contested Terrain: The IAMAW unionization drive at the L3Harris SRM plant in Camden is the most significant and actionable vulnerability identified. Supporting this labor struggle is now a primary strategic imperative for the anti-war movement. A successful unionization effort that builds anti-war solidarity could create a material disruption at the most critical chokepoint in the entire propulsion supply chain.
  • Specialized Production as a Weakness: Companies like Moog Inc. are indispensable due to their specialization. This also makes them uniquely vulnerable. Unlike a massive, diversified conglomerate like RTX, a company focused on a niche technology is more sensitive to reputational damage and consumer pressure that could affect its other business lines.

By deconstructing the supply chain, we deconstruct the illusion of an invulnerable, monolithic military-industrial complex. This investigation provides the intelligence necessary to wage a more precise, more targeted, and more effective struggle against the material enablers of Israeli apartheid.
